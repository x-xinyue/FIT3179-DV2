How does Malaysia compare to other countries?

Inflation in Malaysia has generally remained stable compared to its regional neighbours. Between 2019 and 2024, Malaysia's inflation rose sharply in 2021 following reopening of borders and global supply disruptions but stayed below the levels recorded in countries such as Singapore and the Philippines.

What’s Driving Malaysia’s Inflation?

The streamgraph shows the breakdown of Malaysia’s inflation by groups. The movement of each category generally aligns with the national inflation pattern. The largest area between 2022 and 2023 highlights the peak of inflationary pressure, suggesting that price increases were most widespread and intense during that period.

Top 5 Contributing Inflation (%) Groups in Malaysia

Food and Beverages have been the most persistent drivers of inflation, consistently ranking among the top five highest-inflation categories each year. In 2023, this category recorded the highest inflation rate, reinforcing its dominant role in overall price growth.

Where Does Inflation Hit Malaysians the Hardest?

Urbanised states such as Selangor and Kuala Lumpur experienced higher price increases, while northern states like Perlis and Kelantan remained relatively stable.

How Affordable Are Basic Foods in Malaysia?

The Malaysian government regulates several essential food items to stabilise living costs. Despite global commodity price shocks, items such as sugar and flour have shown limited price increases. However, rice prices continue to increase despite heavy regularization.

Can Malaysia Produce Enough to Feed Itself?

Domestic food production plays a key role in price stability. Malaysia’s poultry production recovered strongly post-pandemic, but rice output has stagnated due to land constraints and weather variability. Strengthening local production capacity could reduce future dependence on imports and mitigate food inflation risks.
